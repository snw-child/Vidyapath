What is a Set?

A set is a well-defined collection of objects. Each object in a set is called an element or member.

📌 A set must be well-defined — we can clearly say whether any object belongs or not.

Notation

Sets are named with capital letters and elements written inside curly braces { }.

A = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}

We say 1 ∈ A (1 is a member of A) and 6 ∉ A (6 is not a member of A).

Types of Sets

Empty Set (∅)
No elements. Written { } or ∅. Example: months with 32 days.
Finite Set
Countable elements. Example: {2,4,6,8,10}
Infinite Set
Goes on forever. Example: {1,2,3,4,...}
Universal Set (U)
Contains ALL elements being considered.

Ways to Describe a Set

Roster Method: List all elements: B = {a, e, i, o, u}

Description Method: B = {vowels in English alphabet}

Equal Sets

✅ {1,2,3} = {3,1,2} — Same elements, order does not matter!
⚡ Key Points
Elements never repeated in a set
Order of elements does not matter
∈ means "is a member of", ∉ means "is not a member of"

What is a Fraction?

A fraction represents a part of a whole.

Numerator / Denominator

The numerator (top) tells how many parts we have. The denominator (bottom) tells how many equal parts the whole is divided into.

Types of Fractions

Proper Fraction
Numerator less than denominator. eg: 3/4
Improper Fraction
Numerator ≥ denominator. eg: 7/4
Mixed Number
Whole number + fraction. eg: 2½
Like Fractions
Same denominator. eg: 1/5 and 3/5

Adding Like Fractions

Same denominators — just add numerators!
1/7 + 3/7 = 4/7

Adding Unlike Fractions

Steps: 1. Find LCD (Lowest Common Denominator)
2. Convert each fraction to LCD
3. Add numerators
4. Simplify

Example: 1/3 + 1/4. LCD = 12. So 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

Subtracting Fractions

5/6 – 1/4: LCD=12. 10/12 – 3/12 = 7/12
⚡ Key Rules
Always simplify your answer
LCD = LCM of the denominators
Convert improper fractions to mixed numbers when needed

What are Decimals?

A decimal uses a dot (decimal point) to represent fractions.

0.5 = 5/10 = one-half

Place Value

Digit positionName
1st after decimalTenths (÷10)
2nd after decimalHundredths (÷100)
3rd after decimalThousandths (÷1000)

Comparing Decimals

1. Line up decimal points
2. Compare digits left to right
3. First difference decides which is larger
0.45 vs 0.5 → tenths: 4 vs 5 → 0.5 is larger

Adding & Subtracting

Golden rule: Line up decimal points!
3.25 + 1.40 = 4.65

Multiplying

Multiply as whole numbers, then count total decimal places.

1.2 × 0.3 = 0.36 (2 decimal places)

Converting

Fraction → Decimal
Divide numerator by denominator. 1/4 = 0.25
Decimal → Fraction
Write over power of 10 and simplify. 0.75 = 3/4

What is a Percentage?

"Percent" means per hundred — out of 100.

50% = 50/100 = 0.5 = ½

Common Conversions

FractionDecimal%
1/20.550%
1/40.2525%
3/40.7575%
1/50.220%
1/100.110%

Finding a % of a Number

30% of 150 = (30÷100) × 150 = 0.30 × 150 = 45

Finding What % One Number is of Another

% = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
15 out of 60 = (15÷60)×100 = 25%

Quick Shortcuts

10% = divide by 10
25% = divide by 4
50% = divide by 2

Factors

A factor divides a number exactly (no remainder).

Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

Multiples

Multiples are what you get multiplying by 1, 2, 3, ...

Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25...

Prime Numbers

Exactly two factors: 1 and itself.

Primes: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19...
Note: 1 is NOT prime. 2 is the only even prime.

HCF — Highest Common Factor

HCF of 12 and 18:
Factors of 12: {1,2,3,4,6,12}
Factors of 18: {1,2,3,6,9,18}
HCF = 6

LCM — Lowest Common Multiple

LCM of 4 and 6:
Multiples of 4: 4,8,12...
Multiples of 6: 6,12...
LCM = 12
💡 We use LCM to find LCD when adding fractions!

Prime Factorisation

24 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 2³ × 3

What is Matter?

Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. Everything around us is made of matter.

States of Matter

🧊 Solid
Definite shape and volume. Particles tightly packed. eg: ice, rock, wood.
💧 Liquid
Definite volume, no fixed shape. Takes shape of container. eg: water, oil.
💨 Gas
No fixed shape or volume. Fills any container. eg: air, steam.

Changes of State

Melting
Solid → Liquid (heat added). Ice melts at 0°C.
Freezing
Liquid → Solid (heat removed). Water freezes at 0°C.
Evaporation / Boiling
Liquid → Gas (heat added). Water boils at 100°C.
Condensation
Gas → Liquid (heat removed). Steam on a cold surface.
Sublimation
Solid → Gas directly. Example: dry ice (solid CO₂).

Physical vs Chemical Properties

💡 Physical change is reversible (melting ice). Chemical change produces a new substance (burning wood).

7 Characteristics — MRSGREN

M – Movement
All living things move (plants turn toward light).
R – Respiration
Converting food into energy.
S – Sensitivity
Responding to environment (light, heat, touch).
G – Growth
All living things grow over time.
R – Reproduction
Producing offspring.
E – Excretion
Removing waste made inside the body.
N – Nutrition
Taking in food and water for energy and growth.

Needs of Living Things

🌱 Plants: Water, sunlight, CO₂, minerals
🐾 Animals: Food, water, oxygen, shelter, space

Kingdoms

Animal Kingdom
Cannot make own food. Move. No cell walls. eg: dogs, birds, fish.
Plant Kingdom
Make food by photosynthesis. Have cell walls. eg: trees, flowers.
Fungi Kingdom
Absorb nutrients from dead matter. eg: mushrooms.
Bacteria Kingdom
Single-celled, no nucleus. Some helpful, some harmful.
Vertebrates
Animals WITH a backbone. Mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, amphibians.
Invertebrates
Animals WITHOUT a backbone. Insects, worms, crabs, jellyfish.

What is Photosynthesis?

Plants make their own food using sunlight, water, and CO₂.

The Equation

6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
Carbon dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen
(using energy from Sunlight)

Key Structures

🌿 Chloroplasts
Tiny green structures in leaf cells where photosynthesis happens.
🟢 Chlorophyll
Green pigment that captures light energy from the sun.
💨 Stomata
Tiny pores on leaf underside. CO₂ enters, O₂ exits.
🌡️ Glucose
Sugar produced during photosynthesis. Used for energy or stored as starch.

Inputs and Outputs

InputsOutputs
💧 Water (from roots)🍬 Glucose (food for plant)
🌬️ CO₂ (from air via stomata)💨 Oxygen (released to air)
☀️ Light energy (from sun)

Why is Photosynthesis Important?

✅ Produces food — base of all food chains
✅ Releases oxygen animals breathe
✅ Removes CO₂ from atmosphere

What is Digestion?

Breaking down food into small molecules the blood can absorb and cells can use.

The Digestive Journey

1. Mouth
Teeth chew (mechanical). Saliva with amylase begins starch digestion. Food = bolus.
2. Oesophagus
Carries food by PERISTALSIS (wave-like muscle contractions).
3. Stomach
Muscles churn food. Gastric acid kills bacteria. Pepsin digests proteins. Food = chyme.
4. Small Intestine
Most digestion and ALL absorption here. VILLI (finger-like projections) absorb nutrients into blood.
5. Large Intestine
Water re-absorbed. Waste formed into faeces.
6. Rectum & Anus
Faeces stored then expelled.

Digestive Enzymes

LipasePancreasFats → Fatty Acids
Liver
Produces bile to emulsify (break up) fats.

Wave-like muscle contractions moving food along.

What is History?

People have lived on Earth for a very long time — living in different places, building communities, and creating relationships. History is the story describing these past events.

📌 History means studying and describing the details of people who lived in the past.

Sources

Anything that helps us learn history is called a source.

📚 Literary Sources
Ancient written records. eg: Deepawamsa, Mahawamsa.
🏺 Archaeological Sources
Physical remains — ruins, coins, objects. eg: Galvihara, Polonnaruwa.

Why Study History?

Sri Lanka was called the "Granary of the East" for its successful farming. Studying the past helps us solve present environmental problems and plan a better future!

Time Systems

B.C. (Before Christ)
Before birth of Jesus. Counts backwards — 100 BC is further than 50 BC.
A.D. (Anno Domini)
After birth of Jesus. Counts forward.
Buddhist Era
Starts from death of Lord Buddha — 544 BC. Add 544 to BC date to get Buddhist Era.
Hijri Calendar
Starts 622 AD — when Prophet Muhammad migrated from Mecca to Madeena.

Origin of Earth

Our earth is about 4,540 million years old, formed from a cloud of dust (nebula). Life first appeared in water.

The Great Ice Age

About 2.5 million years ago, the Earth became extremely cold — the Great Ice Age. Ice covered northern and southern regions. Food was scarce, forcing human ancestors to adapt. It had 4 very cold and 4 short warm periods.

Human Evolution

Australopithecus (3.8 million years ago)
Earliest human ancestor. First to walk on two feet. Used rounded stones as tools.
Homo Habilis (2.3–1.4 million years ago)
"Capable man." First to make sharpened stone tools.
Homo Erectus (1.8 million years ago)
First to use FIRE! Evidence found in Israel dating 790,000 years.
Homo Neanderthalensis (600,000 years ago)
Built houses from animal bones. Ate cooked vegetables. Good hunters.
Homo Sapien (100,000 years ago)
Modern humans! Discovered language, music, painting, dancing. All humans today are Homo Sapiens.

Stone Age Eras

🪨 Paleolithic (Old Stone Age)
Hunted and gathered. Lived in caves. Used red ochre for art. Groups of 20–30.
🏹 Mesolithic (Middle Stone Age)
Started ~20,000 years ago. Invented bows and arrows. Drew rock paintings.
🌾 Neolithic (New Stone Age)
Started ~9,000 years ago. Began AGRICULTURE! Tamed animals, made pottery. First villages ~6,000 BC.

What is a Civilization?

A civilization is a complex society with cities, writing, organized government, religious buildings, and trade. The oldest civilizations grew near rivers for water and farming.

🌍 Four Ancient River Civilizations
Mesopotamia (Euphrates & Tigris) · Egypt (Nile) · Indus Valley (Indus River) · Hwang-Ho (Yellow River, China)

Mesopotamian Civilization (3,100–2,900 BC)

Between Euphrates and Tigris rivers (modern Iraq). "Country between two rivers."

📝 Cuneiform
World's OLDEST writing system. Written on wet clay with a stick. Started as pictures, evolved into symbols.
⚖️ Hammurabi's Law
Oldest law book in the world, written by King Hammurabi.
🏛️ Ziggurats
Temple towers for worshipping gods. Each city had its own god.
🔢 Maths Gifts
360° in a circle, 60 seconds in a minute, 60 minutes in an hour!

Egyptian Civilization (from 3,150 BC)

Along the Nile River. Ruled by Pharaohs.

Pyramids: Tombs for Pharaohs. Pyramids at Giza — one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
Hieroglyphics: Picture writing system using animals and everyday objects.
Mummification: Preserving dead bodies for thousands of years.

Indus Valley Civilization (2,600–1,990 BC)

Along the Indus River in present-day Afghanistan, Pakistan, northwest India.

🏙️ Four Main Cities
Mohenjo-Daro, Harappa, Dholavira, Kalibangan — all well-planned with organized streets.
🚿 Advanced Drainage
Gutters, wells, sewerage more advanced than many cities of that era!
⚡ Gifts of Ancient Civilizations
Mesopotamia: 360° circle, 60-min hour, oldest writing, oldest law
Egypt: Pyramids, mummification, hieroglyphics
Indus Valley: City planning, drainage systems, bronze tools
Hwang-Ho (China): Gunpowder, compass, paper, printing

The First Settlers

About 125,000 years ago, people first arrived in Sri Lanka from a distant country — our earliest ancestors.

First settlers:
• Lived by HUNTING and GATHERING wild food
• Lived in CAVES during rainy seasons
• Used tools made of STONE, bones, and wood
• This early period is called the STONE AGE

Important Cave Sites

🦇 Pahiyangala Cave
Key ancient cave site. Artifacts including oyster shell pendants found here.
🏔️ Kithulgala Cave
Important dwelling used in the hunting era.

Progress Before Prince Vijaya (around 2,400 BC)

Started FARMING on hill plateaus
Discovered how to produce IRON (~4,000 years ago)
Made CLAY POTS and urns
Performed FUNERAL RITES — erected tombs for the dead

Prince Vijaya's Arrival (544 BC)

According to the Mahawamsa, Prince Vijaya and 700 followers arrived from India and landed at Thambapanni on the western coast around 544 BC.

👑 Prince Vijaya's Story
Three groups lived here when Vijaya arrived: Yakka, Naga, and Deva tribes. A Yakka woman named Kuweni (who was spinning cotton) caught Vijaya's followers. He found her, married her, and she helped him become King. His followers built villages including Upatissa Gama and Anuradhagama.

Introduction

Sri Lanka was ruled by brave and wise kings for about 2,000 years. Sailors knew our island as Sihaladeepa, Taprobane, Ceylan, and Serendib. Our kings worked constantly for the welfare of the people.

🏰 King Pandukabhaya

Developed Anuradhagama into the great city of Anuradhapura. Built city gates, roads, ponds, gardens, a hospital, and places of worship for all faiths. Built the first tank near a city — Abhaya Wewa (now Basawakkulama tank). Ruled for 70 years.

🌳 King Devanampiyatissa

During his reign, Arahat Mahinda Thero (son of Emperor Ashoka) brought Buddhism to Sri Lanka. The king embraced Buddhism and built:
• Thuparamaya — first stupa in Sri Lanka (bell-shaped/Ghantakara design)
• Mahamewuna Garden and Maha Vihara

Nun Sangamitta Theri brought the sapling of Sri Maha Bodhi (sacred Bo tree) from India — planted in Mahamewuna Garden.

⚔️ King Dutugemunu

Real name: Gamini Abhaya. Defeated King Elara (righteous Chola king who ruled Anuradhapura 44 years). United the country. Built Ruwanweliseya (Maha Tupa) — 300ft tall, base diameter 298ft. Supported by Ten Great Giants (Dasa Maha Yodhayo) and war elephant Kandula.

📖 King Valagamba

Built Abhayagiri Stupa (350ft tall, base 355ft). Arranged for the Thripitaka (Buddhist scriptures) to be written down for the first time at Aluviharaya temple, Mathale — previously preserved only by memorization. Wife: Queen Soma Devi.

💧 King Vasabha — Lambakarna Dynasty

Built 11 large tanks and 12 canals including the Elahera canal (30 miles long). The Vallipuram gold plate was written during his rule — records a minister building a temple in Jaffna. Ruled 44 years.

🏊 King Mahasen

Built 16 tanks including famous Minneriya tank (1¼ mile bund, 4,670 acres). Built Jethawanaramaya — largest brick stupa in the world (400ft tall, base 370ft). Worshipped today as "Minneri Deviyo". Ruled 27 years.

🌊 King Dhathusena — Maurya Dynasty

Built Kala Wewa and the remarkable Yodha Ela (Jaya Ganga) — a canal 54 miles long connecting Kala Wewa to Tisa Wewa, with a gradient of only 6 inches per mile. Built 18 large and 18 small tanks. Ruled 18 years.

⚡ Quick Reference
First tank near city: Pandukabhaya → Abhaya Wewa
Buddhism arrived: Devanampiyatissa (Arahat Mahinda)
Thripitaka written: Valagamba → Aluviharaya, Mathale
Largest brick stupa: Jethawanaramaya (King Mahasen)
Longest canal: Yodha Ela / Jaya Ganga (Dhathusena) — 54 miles
